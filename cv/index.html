In high school, I dreamt of becoming a concert pianist for the beauty of music and its ability to communicate abstractly. However, somehow I wandered into math, drawn to it by its creative reasoning, its purity, and a desire to understand a subject so vast and deep. I studied music at Brandeis University for a year before transferring to Carnegie Mellon University as a math major. In my undergrad, I dabbled in programming in order to get a job after graduation. Fortunately, the day before Thanksgiving in my senior year, a professor encouraged me to realize that I still prefer math over other career choices. Now I'm a graduate student at University of Nebraska-Lincoln.
